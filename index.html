About us

We like to think of Prague as a fantastical kitchen that contains a myriad of flavours and many hidden wonders and surprises.


Meet to Eat Prague Foodie Tour takes you on an enchanting route through the historical centre that perfectly weaves together local interesting culinary hot spots with traditional Czech dishes. All consumption is included in the price, and whilst the emphasis is upon degustation, you are free to indulge to your own satisfaction.

Savoury and sweet, hot or cold, as locals we open doors to a culinary experience that would otherwise remain closed. Our food and drink pairings and tastes en-route are many and varied, and by the end of the tour you will have satisfied your stomach, excited your taste buds and quenched your thirst while experiencing a private beer tasting in an Old Prague Palace.

Bon Appetit...or as we say “dobrou chut”..

What Is Included

Try our traditional Czech food (cca 14 tastings).

Visit some of the local unique restaurants, pubs and hidden venues.

Sample a selection of superior Czech artisan beer and Moravian wine.

Enjoy locally roasted coffee with delightful Prague artisan desserts and cakes.

Taste a variety of Czech specialty beverages. (Absinth, Becherovka..)

Experience Czech culture, history and architecture along the way.

Make sure you come hungry

Tour Detail

WHEN

We offer 17:15 PM daily tours, rain or shine. The tour lasts approx. 3 hours. The walking distance is 1.2 km.

WHERE

The meeting point and tickets are available in our venue the Craft Beer club U Kunštátů, Řetězová street number 3, Old Town, Prague 1, just a few minutes walk from the famous Astronomical Clock.

HOM MUCH

The cost is 2400 CZK (87 €) per person. The group size is limited to max. 8 people. Advanced booking is recommended.

Craft Beer Club - U Kunstatu

In our venue we offer close to 100 beers from small and mid size independent breweries.

Of Romanesque origins, and dating from around 1180, the palace U Kunstatu is located on a hidden cul-de-sac in the oldest part of the Old Town. It is both a UNESCO protected monument and a ‘time machine’ of architectural styles. The Former palace of George of Podebrady, it was from here that he walked to be crowned King of Bohemia in the year 1458.

The palace has once again opened it’s doors to the public and together with it’s beer garden represents an oasis of tranquility in the middle of Prague.
